Dangers of E-Waste presents: The Overview of E Waste

































E-Waste, or electronic waste, refers to used electronics that have reached the end of their life cycle. These electronics are no longer needed or wanted by the owner. The electronics include cell phones.




































Most E-Waste unfortunatly ends up in landfills. Here, E-Waste amasses which proves dangerous for both communities around the world and the environment.

At The Landfill

At the landfill, the E-Waste sits and is exposed to the elements. Hazardous chemicals in the E-Waste then can leach into groundwater and streams. According to the EPA, every year the world generates around 3,420,000 tons of E-Waste.




































Image Credit: www.geograph.org.uk

In The Ecosystem

In the ecosystem, harmful and toxic chemicals and components leach out of the E-Waste poisioning and endangering the ecosystem. These chemicals end up in the water, groundwater, air, and soil. Animals and plants rely on these sources for survival. The presence of E-Waste in such bodies endangers the plants and animals in the land the water. Chemicals from e-waste also end up in the very crops we consume and drinking water we drink.

In The communities

E-Waste poses a huge threat to communities around the world by affecting drinking water and crops. The problem is even bigger in communities in developing countries such as Guiyu, China. In Guiyu, citizens are exposed to toxic metals and chemicals burning from the landfills of E-Waste. These carcinogens in the air cause major health complications such as cancer, puliminary diseases, and cardiac diseases in many individuals.

In the end, only you can protect the Earth and your community by recycling your E-Waste!
