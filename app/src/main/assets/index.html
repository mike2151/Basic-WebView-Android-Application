INTRODUCTION

     In recent decades, technology has become more prominent than ever. Every day, millions of new electronics are purchased and, as a result, the amount of unwanted and used electronics is rapidly growing. This is because the lifespan for such devices is getting shorter as new and improved technology continually emerges. The concept of "the next best thing" has become commonplace; newer devices with state-of-the-art technology are making their way to consumers. As each iteration of these devices reaches the public, the previous models become increasingly obsolete-causing them to eventually become trash. The problem is so enormous that nearly 250 million computers will become obsolete in the next five years and mobile phones are discarded at a rate of 130 million per year (RecycleIN.gov). Each of these devices houses many uncommon and caustic chemicals which can be destructive to the environment and a danger to human health. If action is not taken in order to mitigate this problem, the number of devices that have become trash will continue to augment, furthering the problems these devices have created.

What is E-Waste?

     E-Waste, or electronic waste, refers to used electronics that have reached the end of their life cycle. These electronics are no longer needed or wanted by the owner. The electronics include, but are not limited to, computers, televisions, VCRs, stereos, batteries, cell phones, copiers, and fax machines.
