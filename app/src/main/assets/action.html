Take Action Today!

      Recycling your used electronics is the best way to counter E-waste all around the world. However, the effort to counter E-waste starts right in your community. To fight E-waste, you can print out and distribute the flyer below to spread awareness. Awareness is the most powerful method in trying to prevent the accumulation of electronics in landfills.


Awareness Flyer For Your Community:


You can also spread awareness by sending this letter to your nearby school or community center about hosting an E-waste week!


E-Waste Week Letter For Your School/Community:


Recycle Today!

The next step for eliminating E-waste in landfills is to directly recycle those unwanted and used electronics. To recycle, we have provided a map of all E-waste recycling facilities in the United States! Simply look up your address to find the nearest E-waste recycling facility. Information is also given about each facility so you can contact them easily and recycle right away. If you are unable to bring the E-waste to the sites, many of the facilities offer pickup services for electronic waste. Also ask about pickup recycling services for office buildings. Your office building may even receive monetary compensation for recycling electronics!


More of the Creative Type?

Instead of recycling, E-waste can also be reused and transformed into art. E-waste art is an emerging craft in which electronics are used as the medium. Ultimately, this helps reduce the amount of E-waste that ends up in landfills and results in beautiful works of art.


A butterfly made from used circuit boards and resistors. Obtained from www.creativespotting.com.


Contact Your Representative

A solution to the harmful effects of not recycling E-waste can also be handled by politics. Currently, the United States is one of the few countries that has not ratified the Basel Convention. It is therefore legal to export E-waste from the United States to developing countries elsewhere in the world. This leads to the massive accumulation of E-waste in landfills which destroy the ecosystems and communities of foreign countries. Write to your representative in congress today to address the lack of reform on such a big issue!
